About Stephen Wenzel

Discovering Photography

Growing up in Clyde, North Carolina—just a stone’s throw from the Blue Ridge Parkway—I found my passion for photography at 15 through exploring the outdoors, finding intricate details in nature that I had never noticed before. It sparked a deep curiosity to explore the world through my lens. Inspired by Joel Sartore’s Photo Ark project, I set out to capture the beauty of animals, plants, and landscapes, and what started as a simple interest quickly became a calling.

The Folkmoot Festival

My love for photography flourished when I volunteered for the Folkmoot International Dance Festival in Waynesville, NC. Photographing international dancers from over 15 countries, I realized that photography wasn’t just about capturing moments—it was about telling stories. The diversity, energy, and emotions of the performers fueled my creative passion, leading me to volunteer and photograph for three consecutive years. I even met friends from the festival on a trip to Romania in 2024!

UNC Chapel Hill & Expanding My Vision

While pursuing my Bachelor of Science in Biology at UNC Chapel Hill, I enrolled in an elective photography course under the guidance of esteemed photographers, including Jeffrey Camarati (Director of Photography, UNC Athletics). This class introduced me to equipment and techniques I had never imagined using. Collaborating with talented classmates and professionals opened my eyes to the limitless possibilities of visual storytelling.

Honing My Craft

Throughout college, I refined my skills through portrait and senior photography sessions, always pushing the boundaries of creativity. Whether experimenting with light painting, shutter drag, or cinematic storytelling, I strive to make each session a collaborative experience, ensuring every client’s vision comes to life.

Community Connection

Photography is more than just an art form—it’s a way to connect and give back. I’ve shared my knowledge by teaching photography to Boy Scout Troop 370 (where I earned my Eagle Scout rank) and serving as the lead photographer & videographer for the Tuscola High School Marching Band. My passion for storytelling has also expanded into videography, adding another layer to my creative work.

Behind the Lens

When I’m not with my camera, you can find me exploring National Parks, working at the Duke Eye Center, or trying new restaurants. I aspire to become a Physician Assistant, with the goal of providing care to areas like my rural hometown of Clyde, North Carolina.

Let's Connect!

I regularly share my latest projects, travel adventures, and creative insights on Instagram and YouTube. My philosophy—Community, Collaboration, Creativity—guides everything I do. Whether you're looking for a classic portrait, a storytelling session, or a bold creative concept, I’d love to bring your vision to life.

Stephen's Philosophy

Visionary Storytelling

Unleashing the power of visual narratives, I craft stories that transcend pixels, capturing the essence of every moment.

Client-Centered Focus

Your vision is my priority. I am dedicated to understanding your unique story and translating it into breathtaking visual art. In addition, I make the process of scheduling, shaping, and attending your photoshoot as client-friendly as possible.

Creative Exploration

I believe in pushing creative boundaries, exploring new perspectives, and infusing every image with artistic expression. After all, the sky is the limit.

Community of Creativiy

Photography is a collaborative art. I engage with a community of creatives, sharing insights and inspiration to continually elevate other photographer's craft in addition to my own.

Community Development

I have volunteered for multiple organizations across North Carolina including the Folkmoot International Dance Festival, the Tuscola High School Band Program, and the Boy Scouts of America. In addition, 10 percent of my profit from nature photography sales directly contributes to upkeep of the trails and overlooks along the Blue Ridge Parkway through the Blue Ridge Parkway Foundation.
