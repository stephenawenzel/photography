About Stephen Wenzel

Discovering Photography

Growing up in Clyde, North Carolina—just a stone’s throw from the Blue Ridge Parkway—I found my passion for photography at 15. One day, a Northern Flicker perched on my windowsill, revealing intricate details I had never noticed before. That moment sparked a deep curiosity to explore the world through my lens. Inspired by Joel Sartore’s Photo Ark project, I set out to capture the beauty of nature, and what started as a simple interest quickly became my calling.

From Landscapes to Portraits: Folkmoot Festival

My love for portrait photography flourished when I volunteered for the Folkmoot International Dance Festival in Waynesville, NC. Photographing dancers from over 15 countries, I realized that photography wasn’t just about capturing moments—it was about telling stories. The diversity, energy, and emotions of the performers fueled my creative passion, leading me to volunteer and photograph for three consecutive years.

UNC Chapel Hill & Expaning My Vision

While pursuing a biology degree at UNC Chapel Hill, I enrolled in an elective photography course under the guidance of Peter Hoffman (UNC Hussman School of Journalism & Media) and Jeffrey Camarati (Director of Photography, UNC Athletics). This class introduced me to equipment and techniques I had never imagined using. Collaborating with talented classmates like Tristan Brown and Elliot Millner opened my eyes to the limitless possibilities of visual storytelling.

Honing My Craft

Throughout college, I refined my skills through portrait and senior photography sessions, always pushing the boundaries of creativity. Whether experimenting with light painting, shutter drag, or cinematic storytelling, I strive to make each session a collaborative experience, ensuring every client’s vision comes to life.

Giving Back to the Community

Photography is more than just an art form—it’s a way to connect and give back. I’ve shared my knowledge by teaching photography to Boy Scout Troop 370 (where I earned my Eagle Scout rank) and serving as the lead photographer & videographer for the Tuscola High School Marching Band. My passion for storytelling has also expanded into videography, adding another layer to my creative work.

Beyond Photography

When I’m not behind the camera, you’ll find me trying adventurous foods (like the Extra Hot Sandwich at Rocky’s Chicken Shack), or exploring National Parks (with over 50 sites stamped in my National Passport!).

Let's Connect!

I regularly share my latest projects, travel adventures, and creative insights on Instagram and YouTube. My philosophy—Community, Collaboration, Creativity—guides everything I do. Whether you're looking for a classic portrait, a storytelling session, or a bold creative concept, I’d love to bring your vision to life.

Stephen's Philosophy

Visionary Storytelling

Unleashing the power of visual narratives, I craft stories that transcend pixels, capturing the essence of every moment.

Client-Centered Focus

Your vision is my priority. I am dedicated to understanding your unique story and translating it into breathtaking visual art. In addition, I make the process of scheduling, shaping, and attending your photoshoot as client-friendly as possible.

Creative Exploration

I believe in pushing creative boundaries, exploring new perspectives, and infusing every image with artistic expression. After all, the sky is the limit.

Community of Creativiy

Photography is a collaborative art. I engage with a community of creatives, sharing insights and inspiration to continually elevate other photographer's craft in addition to my own.

Community Development

I have volunteered for multiple organizations across North Carolina including the Folkmoot International Dance Festival, the Tuscola High School Band Program, and the Boy Scouts of America. In addition, 10 percent of my profit from nature photography sales directly benefit the Blue Ridge Parkway Foundation. This contributes to upkeep of the trails and overlooks along the Blue Ridge. I am also a member of the Waterfall Keepers of North Carolina.
