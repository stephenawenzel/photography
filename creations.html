Locks of Love

A heart-shaped lock stands out on a metal-framed bridge in Cluj -Napoca, Romania, among a myriad of other locks. This tradition, symbolizing eternal love, has roots in European folklore, particularly a tale from early 20th-century Italy. The story tells of a couple who, facing separation, locked a padlock to a bridge and threw away the key as a testament to their unbreakable bond. The practice spread across Europe and globally, with bridges in cities like Paris becoming famous for their adorned locks. In Cluj-Napoca, this modern custom continues to transform bridges into romantic landmarks, where love stories are symbolically sealed for eternity.
